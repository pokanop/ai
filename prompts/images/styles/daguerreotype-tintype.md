# Daguerreotype / Tintype

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

The earliest photographic processes — portraits on mirror-polished silver plates (daguerreotype) or dark lacquered metal (tintype/ferrotype). The unique qualities: mirror-like reflective surfaces that shift between positive and negative depending on viewing angle, extraordinarily fine detail from the plateless grain, long exposure motion blur on anything that moved, and the haunting, ghostly presence of subjects captured at the very dawn of photography. These images feel like windows into the past.

**Best for:** Art prints · Social media posts · Portrait photography · Historical content · Desktop wallpapers · Conceptual art

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices
- **Mirror surface**: "Mirror-polished silver plate" with reflectivity is the daguerreotype's signature — it's unique in all photography.
- **Motion blur from long exposure**: Hands, clothing that moved during the long exposure should be slightly blurred.
- **Brass mat and leather case**: The housing is part of the aesthetic — always include it.
- **Daguerreotype ≠ tintype**: Daguerreotype = reflective silver, extremely fine detail. Tintype = matte dark metal, slightly rough. They're different processes.
