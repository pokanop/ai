# Persian Miniature

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Richly detailed paintings from the Persian manuscript tradition — flat perspective, jewel-toned mineral pigments, gold leaf accents, intricate floral borders, and narrative scenes depicting gardens, courts, hunts, and legends. Persian miniature painting combines microscopic detail (every tile pattern, textile weave, and flower petal individually rendered) with flat pictorial space where multiple scenes unfold simultaneously.

**Best for:** Art prints · Social media posts · Home décor · Greeting cards · Book illustrations · Desktop wallpapers

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices
- **Flat perspective**: Persian miniatures do NOT use Western perspective. "Flat perspective with multiple scenes visible simultaneously."
- **Mineral pigment names**: "Lapis lazuli blue, vermillion, malachite green, saffron gold" — these specific pigments define the palette.
- **Microscopic detail**: "Every textile pattern, tile design, and flower petal individually rendered" — the density of detail is the hallmark.
- **Gold leaf accents**: Essential for the luminous, precious quality.
- **Pairs well with:** [Ukiyo-e Woodblock](ukiyo-e-woodblock.md) (similar flat perspective, different culture), [Illuminated Manuscript](illuminated-manuscript.md) (similar manuscript tradition)
