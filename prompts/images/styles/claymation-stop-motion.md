# Claymation / Stop-Motion

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Handcrafted clay characters and miniature sets with visible fingerprint impressions, slightly imperfect posing, and warm tungsten lighting. Evokes the tactile charm of Wallace & Gromit, Coraline, and Laika Studios productions — where every surface tells you a human hand shaped it. The style's magic is in the imperfections: fingerprints in the clay, seams where pieces join, slightly uneven surfaces, and the warm, intimate scale of miniature sets.

**Best for:** Social media posts · Character designs · Children's content · Desktop wallpapers · Greeting cards · Product mockups · Animated series concepts

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Fingerprints are the signature**: "Visible fingerprint impressions and sculpting tool marks" is non-negotiable. Without it, you get smooth 3D that looks nothing like real clay.
- **Imperfections = charm**: "Slightly uneven surfaces, visible seams where clay pieces join" communicates handmade authenticity. Perfection is the enemy of claymation.
- **Tungsten = clay world lighting**: "Warm tungsten lighting" is the canonical light source for clay animation. Cool or neutral lighting makes clay look medical.
- **Shallow depth of field**: Real stop-motion always has dramatic shallow focus because the subjects are tiny. Never skip this.
- **Reference the right studio**: "Aardman" produces cheerful, comedic clay (Wallace & Gromit). "Laika" produces darker, more detailed clay (Coraline, Kubo). Choose based on mood.
- **Common pitfalls**: Avoid "plasticine" as the only descriptor — include "fingerprints" and "tool marks." Don't mix with "smooth" or "polished" — they override the clay imperfections.
