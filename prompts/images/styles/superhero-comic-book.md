# Superhero Comic Book

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Dynamic action panels with heavy ink outlines, halftone dot shading, speed lines, and dramatic foreshortening. This is the visual language of Marvel and DC's Silver and Bronze Age — bold, kinetic, and unapologetically dramatic. Characters burst from the frame with exaggerated musculature and flowing capes, while onomatopoeic sound effects ("BOOM!", "CRACK!") float in stylized lettering. The style applies equally well to original characters, real-person conversions, and scene compositions.

**Best for:** Social media posts · Profile pictures · Character designs · Poster prints · Birthday invitations · Fan art

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Halftone dots are the signature**: "Classic halftone dot shading" is what separates comic book art from generic illustration. Always include it.
- **Name the era**: "Silver Age Marvel/DC" produces classic bold heroics. "Modern Image Comics" produces darker, grittier results. "Golden Age" produces simpler, more wholesome compositions.
- **Foreshortening adds power**: "Dramatic foreshortening on the leading fist" or "low camera angle looking up" immediately adds dynamism.
- **Onomatopoeia sells it**: Adding "BOOM!", "CRACK!", or "WHOOSH!" in the prompt creates authentic comic book sound effects that cement the style.
- **Common pitfalls**: Avoid mixing "realistic" with comic book — it produces an awkward painted look. Don't skip "heavy black ink outlines" — without them it looks like generic illustration.
