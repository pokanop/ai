# Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead)

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

The vibrant visual language of Mexico's Day of the Dead celebration — sugar skull (calavera) face painting with intricate floral patterns, marigold (cempasúchil) flowers, papel picado banners, ofrendas (altars with candles and photos), and a palette that celebrates life through death's imagery. Every surface is decorated — faces become canvases, altars overflow with color, and the boundary between life and the spirit world is joyfully blurred.

**Best for:** Social media posts · Profile pictures · Art prints · Greeting cards · Desktop wallpapers · Poster prints · Halloween/cultural content

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices
- **Sugar skull face paint details**: "Floral patterns around the eyes, painted nose as inverted heart, decorative line work" — specificity matters.
- **Marigolds (cempasúchil)**: The signature flower. "Vibrant orange marigolds" should appear prominently.
- **Papel picado**: "Intricately cut papel picado banners" add the festive cultural texture.
- **Celebrate, don't mourn**: The tone is joyful, vibrant, and celebratory — honoring the departed with color and life.
