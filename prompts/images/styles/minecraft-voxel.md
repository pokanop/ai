# Minecraft / Voxel

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Blocky cubic geometry, visible voxel grids, and RTX ray-traced lighting in the Minecraft visual language. Everything is constructed from cubes — terrain, characters, animals, structures — but the lighting is cinematic, with dynamic shadows, volumetric fog, and realistic reflections on water. The contrast between the primitive geometry and the sophisticated lighting is what makes this style so visually striking.

**Best for:** Desktop wallpapers · Gaming content · YouTube thumbnails · Social media posts · Phone wallpapers · Twitch banners

![Minecraft Example](../../../assets/placeholder-minecraft.jpeg)

> **Sample prompt used to generate the above image (Nano Banana 2):**
> ```text
> Minecraft-style voxel render of a cozy village nestled in a spruce forest at sunset, 16:9 landscape format. Blocky cubic geometry — every element composed of visible voxel cubes. Cobblestone paths, oak-plank market stalls with colored wool awnings, lantern posts emitting warm light. RTX ray-traced lighting with golden sunset rays casting long block-shaped shadows through the trees. Volumetric fog drifting between the buildings. Reflective puddles on the cobblestone catching the sky colors. Cinematic wide-angle composition.
> ```

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **The duality is the style**: The magic is the contrast between primitive cubic geometry and cinematic RTX lighting. Always include both elements.
- **Block textures matter**: Reference specific Minecraft block names — "oak planks," "cobblestone," "obsidian," "glowstone" — for authentic textures.
- **Prevent smoothing**: Without explicit "blocky cubic geometry" language, AI models tend to smooth blocks into rounded voxels. Be forceful about cubes.
- **Lighting is 80% of the mood**: "Sunset through spruce trees" and "torch-lit cave" and "underwater elder guardian chamber" are all Minecraft — the lighting changes everything.
- **Common pitfalls**: Avoid "Minecraft-inspired" (too loose) — specify "Minecraft-style voxel" with explicit block geometry. Don't ask for realistic proportions on characters — embrace the rectangular body and square head.
- **Pairs well with:** [Isometric Pixel City](isometric-pixel-city.md) (similar blocky aesthetic, different perspective), [Lego Photography](lego-photography.md) (similar toy-world charm)
