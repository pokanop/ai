# Art Deco Illustration

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Gatsby-era geometric elegance with metallic gold and black palettes, radiating sunburst patterns, streamlined stylized figures, and symmetrical compositions. The visual language of 1920s luxury — from the Chrysler Building's crown to vintage Champagne posters to Tamara de Lempicka's gleaming portraits. Art Deco celebrates machine-age optimism, opulence, and precision geometry. Every element follows strict symmetry, strong verticals, and repeating geometric motifs: chevrons, zigzags, stepped pyramids, and radiating sunbursts.

**Best for:** Poster prints · Event invitations · Brand assets · Social media posts · Book covers · Menu designs · Wedding stationery

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Symmetry is sacred**: "Strict symmetrical composition" is non-negotiable. Art Deco without symmetry is just illustration.
- **Limit the palette**: 3-4 colors maximum. Gold + black + cream + one accent is the classic Deco formula.
- **Metallic gold texture**: "Metallic gold foil texture on all gold elements" is what makes Deco feel luxurious rather than flat.
- **Geometric borders frame everything**: "Chevrons, zigzags, and stepped pyramid motifs" in the border authenticate the style.
- **Common pitfalls**: "Art Deco" alone often produces Art Nouveau (organic, flowing) instead. Always include "geometric" and "symmetrical" to anchor it. Avoid organic curves — Deco is angular and mechanical.
- **Pairs well with:** [Soviet Constructivist](soviet-constructivist.md) (same era, different ideology and palette), [Wes Anderson Symmetry](wes-anderson-symmetry.md) (shares obsessive composition, warmer palette)
