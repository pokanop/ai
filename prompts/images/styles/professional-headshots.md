# Professional Headshots

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Polished, corporate-grade portrait photography optimized for business and professional contexts. Unlike cinematic headshots that lean into dramatic lighting and moody aesthetics, professional headshots prioritize clean, even lighting, neutral backgrounds, and a composed, approachable expression — the standard expected by Fortune 500 companies, law firms, consulting agencies, and executive teams. These prompts produce images that look like they came from a $500 studio session.

**Best for:** LinkedIn profiles · Corporate team pages · Executive bios · Business cards · Conference speaker profiles · Company websites · Email signatures · Board of Directors pages

> Professional business headshot photograph of an executive woman in her 40s wearing a tailored charcoal blazer over an ivory silk blouse, 4:5 vertical portrait format. 85mm portrait lens at f/2.0. Sharp focus on the eyes, natural skin texture with visible pores, warm confident smile with closed lips. Hair neatly styled. Subtle catchlights in both eyes.
> ```

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Attire matters**: Dark solid-color blazers (navy, charcoal) photograph best. Avoid busy patterns, logos, and bright whites which blow out under studio lighting. A pop of color at the neckline (a blouse, tie, or scarf) adds visual interest without distraction.
- **Background choice signals context**: Grey = versatile corporate. White = clean/modern. Dark/charcoal = premium/executive. Branded colors = marketing use. Match the background to where the headshot will be used.
- **Lighting ratio is key**: For business headshots, keep the key-to-fill ratio between 2:1 and 3:1. Higher ratios (4:1+) create dramatic shadows that read as "artistic" rather than "professional." Specify the ratio in your prompt.
- **Expression coaching**: "Warm, approachable expression with a slight smile" produces the most universally effective business headshot. Avoid "serious" or "stern" — these produce unfriendly results. Avoid "big smile" — this reads as casual.
- **Catchlights**: Include "subtle catchlights in both eyes" — these small reflections of the light source make the eyes look alive and engaged. Without them, the portrait looks flat.
- **Consistency for team pages**: When generating a set of headshots for a team page, specify identical lighting, backdrop, crop, and format across all prompts. Use NB2's multi-turn: generate the first headshot, then say "Create another headshot in exactly the same lighting and backdrop setup for [next person]."
- **Square vs. 4:5**: Use `1:1` for profile pictures and directories. Use `4:5` for team pages, LinkedIn, and editorial contexts where the extra vertical space adds gravitas.
- **Common pitfalls**: Avoid "beautiful," "handsome," or "attractive" — these bias the output toward idealized features. Describe specific professional attributes instead. Avoid "selfie" — this produces a phone-camera look with wide-angle distortion.
