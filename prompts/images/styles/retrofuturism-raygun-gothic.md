# Retrofuturism / Raygun Gothic

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

The retro-future that never happened — chrome rocketships, bubble-domed cities, finned automobiles, and the boundless technological optimism of 1950s/60s science fiction. The visual language of *The Jetsons*, *Forbidden Planet*, and *Googie architecture* — where every surface is streamlined, every vehicle has fins, and the future is bright, chrome, and atomic-powered.

**Best for:** Desktop wallpapers · Social media posts · Poster prints · Concept art · Brand imagery · Editorial illustrations · T-shirt designs

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Chrome + pastels = retrofuturism**: Everything metallic is chrome. Everything else is mid-century pastel — coral, mint, butter yellow, powder blue.
- **No sharp edges**: "Streamlined, rounded shapes" — retrofuturism is aerodynamic even when something doesn't need to fly.
- **Atomic motifs**: Sunbursts, starbursts, and atom symbols are the decorative language of the era.
- **Optimism is the mood**: This is NOT dystopian sci-fi. Everything is bright, sunny, and hopeful. The future is wonderful.
- **Common pitfalls**: Don't mix with cyberpunk or brutalism — those are opposite aesthetics. Avoid gritty or dark tones.
- **Pairs well with:** [Steampunk Contraptions](steampunk-contraptions.md) (alt-history technology, different era), [Art Deco Illustration](art-deco-illustration.md) (similar geometric elegance)
