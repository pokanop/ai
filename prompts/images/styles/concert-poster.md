# Concert Poster / Gig Poster

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Screen-printed aesthetic with limited 3–4 color palettes, heavy ink texture, hand-drawn typography, and bold graphic compositions. Inspired by silkscreen artists like Shepard Fairey, Aesthetic Apparatus, and the Fillmore poster tradition. These posters are designed to be collectible art — each one a unique graphic statement layered with halftone dots, ink bleed, and the tactile warmth of paper stock showing through transparent ink layers.

**Best for:** Event posters · Social media announcements · Print art · Band merchandise · Festival branding · Wall art

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Limit the palette strictly**: "Limited 4-color screen print" is more effective than naming colors alone. Real screen printing is limited by physical ink layers.
- **Paper stock matters**: "Kraft paper," "cream cotton," "newsprint" — the paper shows through transparent ink areas and dramatically affects the look.
- **Halftone is the texture**: "Halftone dots in gradient areas" creates the physical printing feel. Without this, it looks like a flat digital illustration.
- **Typography IS the design**: Concert posters live and die by their lettering. Specify the style: "hand-drawn psychedelic," "bold condensed sans-serif," "Art Nouveau display."
- **Common pitfalls**: "Poster" alone is too generic. Always specify "screen-printed" and name the ink count. Don't use photographic language — this is graphic illustration.
