# Brutalist Architecture

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Monolithic raw concrete forms with dramatic shadows, geometric repetition, and uncompromising structural honesty. Brutalism finds beauty in the exposed material — béton brut (raw concrete) — where massive geometric volumes create dramatic interplays of light and shadow. The style celebrates structural honesty over decoration, producing images that feel simultaneously oppressive and awe-inspiring, ancient and futuristic.

**Best for:** Desktop wallpapers · Art prints · Social media posts · Architecture concept art · Editorial illustrations · Album covers

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **"Raw béton brut concrete"**: This is the specific material name. "Concrete" alone might produce smooth modern concrete. Béton brut has formwork lines and aggregate texture.
- **Shadows are the art**: Brutalist buildings become interesting through shadow play. Always specify sun angle and shadow intensity.
- **Scale reference**: Include "a human figure at the base for scale" — brutalism's power is in its monumental scale, which only reads when there's a size reference.
- **Geometric repetition**: The rhythmic repetition of identical structural elements (windows, balconies, columns) is a core Brutalist motif.
- **Common pitfalls**: Don't over-decorate — brutalism is anti-decoration. Avoid "beautiful" or "elegant" — the aesthetic is intentionally severe. Don't add color — the power is in the monochromatic concrete.
- **Pairs well with:** [Solarpunk](solarpunk.md) (nature + architecture, opposite ideology), [Cyberpunk Noir](cyberpunk-noir.md) (similar dystopian monumental scale)
