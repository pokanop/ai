# Vinyl Album Cover Art

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Classic album cover compositions designed for the square vinyl format — bold, iconic single-image compositions with genre-specific aesthetics. From Blue Note jazz noir to psychedelic rock swirls to the geometric precision of Kraftwerk's electronic covers, album art is one of the most recognized forms of commercial illustration. The format's square discipline (12" × 12") forces strong, centered compositions that read at both full size and thumbnail.

**Best for:** Album covers · Playlist covers · Social media posts · Poster prints · Music branding · Spotify/Apple Music artwork

Inspired by the iconic Blue Note Records cover photography of Francis Wolff. Bold sans-serif typography reading "MIDNIGHT IN BLUE" at the top in cream white.
> ```

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Name the genre tradition**: "Blue Note Records" (jazz), "Storm Thorgerson" (prog rock), "Peter Saville" (electronic), "Victor Moscoso" (psychedelic) — these references give the AI a specific visual tradition to follow.
- **Square is non-negotiable**: Always use `1:1`. Album art lives in a square.
- **Typography matters**: Specify the font style (condensed sans-serif, hand-drawn psychedelic, minimal Helvetica) and placement. Typography is part of the composition.
- **Film grain authenticates**: "Subtle film grain texture throughout" adds the analog warmth that makes album art feel real.
- **Common pitfalls**: Don't skip the genre reference — "album cover" alone produces generic results. Don't place text as an afterthought — it should be designed into the composition.
