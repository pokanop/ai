# Bioluminescent Underwater

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

The otherworldly glow of deep-sea bioluminescence — creatures and environments lit by their own internal light in the absolute darkness of the deep ocean. Jellyfish pulse with electric blue and violet, anglerfish lures glow in the void, and bioluminescent plankton turn ocean waves into liquid starlight. The palette is dominated by deep blacks and blues accented with vivid cyan, violet, green, and warm amber bioluminescent glows.

**Best for:** Desktop wallpapers · Phone wallpapers · Social media posts · Art prints · Album covers · Conceptual art

> **Sample prompt used to generate the above image (Nano Banana 2):**
> ```text
> Underwater photograph of a bioluminescent jellyfish in the deep ocean, 9:16 vertical phone wallpaper format. The jellyfish's bell pulses with electric blue and violet bioluminescent light, illuminating its own translucent tissues from within. Long trailing tentacles glow with dot-lines of cyan light. The surrounding water is absolute deep-ocean black except where the jellyfish's light scatters in a soft halo. Tiny bioluminescent plankton particles sparkle like stars in the water around it. The creature appears to float in infinite dark space — alien, beautiful, ethereal.
> ```

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices
- **Absolute darkness**: The background must be "deep-ocean black" for bioluminescence to work visually.
- **Internal light source**: Bioluminescent creatures light themselves — don't add external lighting.
- **Plankton particles**: "Tiny bioluminescent plankton sparkling like stars in the water" adds depth and magic.
- **Color specificity**: "Electric blue and violet" is the classic bioluminescent palette. Warm amber for anglerfish lures.
