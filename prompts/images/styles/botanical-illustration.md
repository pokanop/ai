# Botanical Illustration

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Precise, scientific illustration with watercolor on cream paper — labeled cross-sections, Latin script annotations, and pressed-specimen aesthetics. Inspired by the incomparable works of Ernst Haeckel, Maria Sibylla Merian, and the great naturalist illustrators of the 18th–19th centuries.

![Botanical Illustration Example](../../../assets/placeholder-botanical.jpeg)

> **Sample prompt used to generate the above image (Nano Banana 2):**
> ```text
> Scientific botanical illustration of a passionflower (Passiflora incarnata) on aged cream watercolor paper, 4:5 vertical format. The main specimen shows a flowering branch with three stages of bloom — bud, full flower, and fruit — connected by a curving vine with tendrils. Detailed inset studies in the corners: a cross-section of the ovary, a magnified view of the corona filaments, and a single seed. Precise watercolor rendering with visible brushstrokes and subtle paper texture. Muted natural pigments — sage green leaves, mauve and white petals, and ochre stems. Hand-lettered Latin binomial and part labels in brown ink. Fine ink outline on all botanical structures. Inspired by Maria Sibylla Merian.
> ```
