# Frosted Glass / Ice Sculpture

[← Back to Image Prompts](../README.md)

Translucent, crystalline subjects carved from clear and frosted ice or glass, with internal refractions, caustic light patterns, condensation droplets, and a cold blue-white palette against dramatic dark backgrounds. The interplay between transparent and frosted surfaces creates stunning optical effects — light splits into rainbow caustics, surfaces bead with condensation, and every edge catches a crisp highlight. The style feels simultaneously elegant, fragile, and powerful.

**Best for:** Desktop wallpapers · Social media posts · Art prints · Product concept renders · Holiday content · Luxury branding

## 💡 Tips & Best Practices

- **Clear vs. frosted contrast**: The magic is in mixing "crystal-clear" and "frosted matte" surfaces on the same sculpture. Pure clear or pure frosted is less visually interesting.
- **Caustic patterns are the wow factor**: "Internal refractions splitting light into rainbow caustic patterns" creates the prismatic light show on the mirrored surface that makes ice sculptures stunning.
- **Condensation sells the cold**: "Fine condensation droplets beading on the surface" is a small detail with huge impact — it makes the viewer *feel* the cold.
- **Dark backgrounds are essential**: Ice sculptures look best against deep charcoal or black. Bright backgrounds wash out the transparency effects.
- **Common pitfalls**: Don't say "frozen" (implies a frozen object, not a carved sculpture). Don't skip the mirrored pedestal — it doubles the caustic light effects.
